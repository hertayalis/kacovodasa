Legal Tussle: Daryl Hall's Lawsuit Against John Oates

American musical duo Hall & Oates (Daryl Hall and John Oates), New York, 1982. (Photo by Luciano Viti/Getty Images)

Recently, the duo found themselves embroiled in a legal dispute. Daryl Hall, aged 77, initiated a lawsuit against his former music partner, John Oates. Court records, accessed by The New York Post, reveal that Hall filed the lawsuit on Nov. 16 in Nashville, Tennessee.

The lawsuit extends beyond the individual artists and delves into their respective trusts — The Daryl Hall Revocable Trust and The John W. Oates TISA Trust. As a result, both trusts, along with their co-trustees, are entangled in the legal proceedings within the Davidson County Chancery Court.

While the nature of the lawsuit is identified as a "contract/debt" matter, the specifics remain undisclosed. Furthermore, the documents are presently sealed. On the heels of this legal saga, a temporary restraining order has been sanctioned by the court, scheduled to take effect from Nov. 30.
