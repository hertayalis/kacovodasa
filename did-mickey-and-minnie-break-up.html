Who is Mickey?

Mickey is an anthropomorphic mouse who has served as The Walt Disney Company’s mascot for many years. He typically wears red shorts, big yellow shoes, and white gloves. 

Walt Disney and Ub Iwerks jointly created the American cartoon character Mickey Mouse in 1928. One of the most well-known and celebrated fictional characters of all time is Mickey.

Mickey is typically portrayed as a sympathetic underdog who survives on grit and cunning in the face of difficulties greater than himself, drawing inspiration from silent film icons like Charlie Chaplin and Douglas Fairbanks.

His short stature and falsetto voice, which were first provided by Disney, serve to personify the character’s representation as a small mouse. 

Mickey was designed to take the place of Oswald the Lucky Rabbit, a former Disney character, and made his first public appearance in the animated film Plane Crazy. 

It was the third Mickey cartoon to be produced, but it was also one of the first to use sound. The name “Mickey” was eventually chosen over the original “Mortimer Mouse” after Lillian Disney’s suggestion on a train ride.

Minnie Mouse, a little history

The Walt Disney Company created the animated character Minnie Mouse. She is an anthropomorphic mouse who has been Mickey Mouse’s longtime love interest. 

She wears white gloves, a red or pink bow, a blue dress with polka dots, white bloomers, and occasionally low-heeled shoes with ribbons. 

She was first given her full name, Minerva Mouse, in the Mickey Mouse comic strip story “The Gleam” by Merrill De Maris and Floyd Gottfredson, though this is rarely used.

Her twin nieces Millie and Melody Mouse and her uncle Mortimer Mouse continue to be her best-known relatives, though Melody only frequently makes an appearance. 

In many appearances, Minnie is presented as the girlfriend of Mickey Mouse and is best friends with Daisy Duck and Clarabelle Cow. 

On January 22, 2018, she received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in recognition of her 90th birthday, joining the ranks of other animated stars.
