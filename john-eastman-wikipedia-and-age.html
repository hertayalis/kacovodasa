John Eastman Wikipedia 

John Charles Eastman was born in 1960 in Lincoln, Nebraska, United States of America. He holds the distinction of being the founding director of the Center for Constitutional Jurisprudence, a public interest law firm associated with the conservative think tank, the Claremont Institute.

His intellectual pursuits and legal career have placed him at the intersection of academia and public discourse.

His early life was marked by a passion for learning and a keen interest in law and politics. He pursued his undergraduate studies at the University of Dallas, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science.

He then attended the University of Chicago Law School, where he obtained his Juris Doctor (J.D.) degree. During his time at law school, Eastman’s intellectual curiosity and commitment to constitutional law began to take shape.

After completing his legal education, Eastman embarked on an academic journey that would establish his reputation as a leading authority on constitutional law.

He taught at various esteemed institutions, including the Chapman University Dale E. Fowler School of Law, where he served as a professor and dean. His scholarship often focused on issues related to federalism, executive power, and constitutional interpretation.

With an impressive background, Eastman previously served as a professor and dean at the Chapman University School of Law. He ventured into politics, running as a Republican candidate for California’s 34th congressional district in 1990 and later contending for the position of California Attorney General in 2010.

Eastman’s legal acumen was further honed during his time as a law clerk to Supreme Court Justice Clarence Thomas.

Eastman gained significant recognition for his legal representation, particularly his role as an attorney for former President Donald Trump. During this period, he attracted attention for suggesting that Vice President Mike Pence possessed the authority to reject the election results from specific states when certifying the outcome of the 2020 United States presidential election.
